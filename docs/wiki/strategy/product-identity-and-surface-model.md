# Product Identity And Surface Model

Updated: 2026-04-14
Status: evolving

## Identity

Intercept is not just an AI news site.

As of the identity pivot confirmed on 2026-04-12, the service is:

- a personal news platform
- driven by user-defined topics
- delivered through an AI character team
- designed so users can intercept, react, and share

The concise framing is:

- "Your news, your way"

This means the AI team is the delivery mechanism, not the subject of the product.

## Product Core

The product has two connected goals:

- publish daily teatime-style conversations that demonstrate the experience
- launch the full service where users define topics and interact with the AI discussion

Core user flow:

1. user chooses or defines a topic
2. the character team turns it into a conversational brief
3. the user intercepts the conversation
4. the result can be kept private or shared publicly

## Character System

The character team remains central to product expression.

Stable english naming used in the product:

- `Ko Bujang`
- `Oh Gwajang`
- `Jem Daeri`

Core roles:

- Ko Bujang: analysis and technical framing
- Oh Gwajang: facts, numbers, planning
- Jem Daeri: community voices and social signals

## Three-Surface Model

A durable product concept from the 2026-04-12 and 2026-04-13 working notes is the three-surface structure:

1. `My Keep` (`/my`)
   - private saved items and personal history
2. `Instant Page` (`/teatime` and related core generation surfaces)
   - the service core where topics become live conversational news
3. `Feed` (`/feed`)
   - the public social layer for shared intercepts

This model should guide navigation, layout, and future product packaging.

## UI Direction

The service is not meant to feel like a generic chat app.

Stable UI direction from working notes:

- carousel or peek-based movement between the three surfaces
- center-focused mobile-first layout
- visible character presence
- social/public layer treated as a moat, not a side feature

## Non-Negotiable Product Rules

- user-defined topics are first-class
- intercepting is a core mechanic, not a side interaction
- private and public outcomes must both feel intentional
- social feed should strengthen product defensibility
- durable positioning should be recorded in the wiki, not left only in campaign copy or meeting notes
